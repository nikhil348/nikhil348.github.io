About

Hi! My name is Nikhil Sharma and I am an aspiring data scientist. I am in my third year at UCLA, where I study Statistics.

On campus, I am involved with Bruin Sports Analytics, where I oversee all operations related to the Data Journalism team as
the Blog Chair. My tasks include editing all articles, advising writers on their analyses and writing pieces of my own. I am
also a member of our club's Research team.

I am also currently working remotely with Dr. Shane Jensen, Associate Professor of Statistics at The Wharton School on a
research project approaching the Ewing Theory in the NBA through statistical modeling. The Ewing Theory is the idea that
when a star athlete leaves a team, the team gets better.

This past summer, I was a Decision Sciences/Business Insights Intern at Communications Media, Inc. in Philadelphia, PA. As an
intern, I was mainly tasked with analyzing and predicting behavior of healthcare professionals for advertising purposes through various NLP and regression models.

In my free time, I enjoy watching NBA games and listening to hip-hop. I occasionally dabble in writing as well; I was once a
Contributor for the Arts & Entertainment section of UCLA's main newspaper, The Daily Bruin.

Feel free to contact me at my email, or my LinkedIn.

Projects
Constructing My All-Time NBA Lineup Through K-Means Clustering
Article, Part 1 Article, Part 2 Code

I wrote the first part of this double-header in November 2018, where I created my best all-time NBA starting lineup with the
help of k-means clustering and random forest concepts in R. I made the visualizations in R using ggplot2 and Plotly. I wrote the
second part in January 2019, where I addressed some criticisms of the first part.

Russell Westbrook: Point Guard or Shooting Guard?
Article Code

I wrote this article in December 2018 with a fellow BSA member. We built a logistic regression model in R that classified
guards as point guards or shooting guards. Then, we ran Russell Westbrook's stats through the model and thoroughly examined
and explained the results. We made the visualizations in R using ggplot2 and base R.

The 3-Point Revolution in the NBA
GIF Code

I created this visualization in September 2018 using the gganimate package in R that shows how dramatically NBA teams have
increased their 3-point shooting throughout the decades. You might recognize this GIF from the front page of this website!

Why LeBron James Left Cleveland

Blake Griffin: Sharpshooter or Bricklayer?

Code
